Informations about blood donation

The importance of blood donation

Donating blood is a noble humanitarian act that helps save the lives of many people suffering from medical emergencies. Each blood donation can save up to three lives, as the blood is separated into its main components: red blood cells, platelets, and plasma.

Adults need to donate blood at least once every three months. The body needs 24 to 48 hours to replace lost fluids and about two weeks to replace red blood cells. Donating blood does not harm the donor; in fact, it stimulates the bone marrow to produce new blood cells.

There is a constant need for blood donations in hospitals to treat emergencies, surgeries, and patients with cancer and chronic diseases. Certain rare blood types are particularly in demand.

The conditions for donating blood are: the donor must be 18-65 years old, weigh over 50 kilograms, be in good health, and not have taken any medications or undergone recent surgeries.

South Lebanon - The Heart of the Community

South Lebanon Governorate is a land of timeless beauty and deep-rooted heritage, stretching from the Mediterranean coast to the rolling hills of the interior. Home to historic cities like Sidon and Tyre, it blends a rich civilizational past with the warmth of a living, breathing community.

The people of South Lebanon are known for their resilience, generosity, and strong bonds of solidarity. Across its villages and cities, a spirit of togetherness defines daily life — making the governorate not just a place, but a community in the truest sense.

The "Hope of South Lebanon" blood donation initiative is one of many humanitarian efforts that reflect the values of this region — a community that stands together, supports one another, and works tirelessly to build a better future for all its people.
